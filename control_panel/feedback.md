# Your notes on the site

Hi Jen — this is your space. It's a guided tour of everything on the site, in plain language, so you can tell us what you'd like changed. You don't need to know anything about code, computers, or websites. You just need to know how it should *feel* and *look*, and you already do — it's your deck.

## How to use this page

- Read through. Each part of the site is explained below.
- Wherever you'd like something different, write it on the **"Your note:"** line under that part. Type however you talk — "make this slower," "I hate this color," "the question should sound warmer." Vague is fine. Strong opinions are great.
- If you love something as-is, leave its line blank. Blank means "don't touch it."
- **Nothing you write here can break anything.** This page is just a notepad. It doesn't change the site by itself. When you're done, hand it back and your notes get turned into the real changes for you.
- Don't worry about spelling, wording, or sounding "technical." If you can describe it, it can be done.

There's a list of all 22 cards near the bottom, in case you want to point at a specific one.

**A lot of this you can also just change yourself** — no waiting, no code. The other files in this folder are plain typing, exactly like this page:

- **`settings.md`** — your contact info, how often cards come up reversed, where the card images live.
- **`appearance.md`** — the colors and the fonts.
- **`words.md`** — the words on screen: the BEGIN button, the title, the omen lines, the button labels.
- **`cards.md`** — every card's title, picture, and all of its writing.

If an edit ever looks wrong, the site quietly falls back to the original — so you genuinely can't break it. Anything you'd rather not touch, just describe below and it'll get done for you.

## 1. The very first thing people see

When someone opens the site, the screen is nearly empty and calm — a soft, warm, parchment-colored background, and a single word in the middle: **BEGIN**. Nothing else. The word gently fades in and out, like it's breathing. When you move the mouse over it (or it's tapped on a phone), it spreads out a little and glows. It's meant to feel quiet and a little ominous, like a held breath before something happens.

## 2. Clicking BEGIN — the smoke and the deck

When someone clicks or taps BEGIN, the word dissolves away and a wisp of **smoke** rises up. Out of that smoke, a small **stack of cards** appears, face-down, like a deck being set on a table. It's slow and deliberate on purpose — it should feel like a real reading is starting, not like a slot machine.

## 3. The card turning over

One card lifts from the deck and **flips over** in the air to reveal its face. Right now, until your artwork is added, the face is a dark card with a gold fleur-de-lis, the card's number, its name, and its old/traditional tarot name. Once your photos are added, the photo fills the card instead.

## 4. Upright or reversed

Like a real reading, the card can come up **upright** or **reversed** (upside-down). It's random, but reversed comes up less often — roughly one time in three. When it's reversed, the picture turns upside-down and the meaning shown is the reversed meaning. A small word on the card says "Upright" or "Reversed" so it's clear.

## 5. The meaning that fades in

Below the card, the writing fades in, in this order:

- **Keywords** — a short row of the card's themes.
- **The reading** — the main paragraph (the upright version, or the reversed version if it came up reversed).
- **The question** — a single italic question meant to land personally (in the deck this is called the *Inquiry*).
- **The tell** — the little sensory detail at the end (the *Environmental Tell*), like a scent or a sound.

All of this is your writing from the guidebook. Every word of it can be changed.

- The **accent** color throughout is gold.

It's meant to feel old, warm, and a little mystical, with the dark cards standing out against the light.
